<p>

Learning how to channel those impulses into the practical application of defending systems has been a gratifying experience on my educational journey.

<h2>CTF Experience Reflection</h2>

<p>  
Participating in CTF competitions has been one of the most valuable parts of my cybersecurity learning experience so far. At first, many of the challenges felt confusing and intimidating, especially when I did not immediately know what tools or techniques to use. However, each challenge helped me get more comfortable with researching, testing ideas, making mistakes, and learning from the results.
</p>

<p>
Through CTFs, I have practiced skills in areas such as:
</p>

<ul>
  <li>Open-source intelligence</li>
  <li>Cryptography</li>
  <li>Password cracking</li>
  <li>Log analysis</li>
  <li>Network traffic analysis</li>
  <li>Web application security</li>
  <li>Forensics</li>
</ul>

<p>
One major lesson I learned is that cybersecurity requires patience and persistence. Sometimes the first approach does not work, and it is important to step back, review the evidence, and try a different method.
</p>
